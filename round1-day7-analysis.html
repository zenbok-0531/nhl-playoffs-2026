The Closeouts.

Carolina is the first team into Round 2 — a 4–2 sweep-clincher in Ottawa that capped a series the Hurricanes never trailed in. But the closeouts everyone expected didn't all come. Minnesota survived in Dallas with an OT goal at the death. Pittsburgh survived Philly with Crosby's first goal of the series. The bracket is closing — and the teams on the wrong side of it are refusing to go quietly.

Round 1 has its first survivor and two refusals. Carolina dispatched Ottawa in four games — a series that on paper looks like a slow-motion blowout but on the ice was three one-goal games and a chippy clincher. Minnesota, facing a 3–1 hole, won on a Boldy deflection with 29 seconds left in OT. Pittsburgh, facing a sweep, finally got their stars to show up — first goals of the series for Crosby and Letang, plus a Silovs goaltending change that paid off. One series ended. Two were saved. The bracket isn't just closing anymore — it's fighting back.

§ 02

Three games, three different fates.

Carolina advanced. Minnesota survived. Pittsburgh survived. The day broke into the patterns Round 1 has been showing all along — structure beats stars, except when stars finally show up.

Series 01 · East  ·  Sweep · Advanced

Carolina at Ottawa

Series Final
Hurricanes 4 — Senators 0
  • Carolina 4, Ottawa 2. Sweep complete. The Hurricanes never trailed in any of the four games — only the 20th team in NHL history to play a series that way, and just the second in the last decade.
  • Game 4 was the most physical of the series — eleven penalties in the second period alone, a Tyler Kleven hit that knocked Alexander Nikishin out of the game, scrums after every whistle. Carolina absorbed all of it without losing structure.
  • Hall opened scoring (his fourth straight game with a point — only the second player in franchise history to start a postseason that way). Batherson tied it on Ottawa's first power-play goal of the series. Stankoven scored the third-period winner — his fourth goal in four games, a franchise first.
  • Aho's two empty-netters bookended a late Cozens goal. Aho now has three career series-clinching goals — the most in Hurricanes/Whalers history.
  • Andersen finished the series with four straight playoff wins, 25 saves on 27 in the clincher, including critical stops while the PK was on the ice for nearly all of the chaotic second.
  • The PK numbers tell the story: Carolina killed 8 of 9 penalties in Game 4 alone, including a 5-on-3. Slavin logged 10:14 of the team's 13:21 shorthanded ice time. That's why this is over.
It was insane, and the guys are playing the game; they're in it, and they understand that this is happening. We've been through all this before. There was zero panic, and I chalk that up to being down this road before. — Rod Brind'Amour, after Game 4
What This Was
A sweep that didn't play like a sweep. Three one-goal games and a chippy clincher, an Ottawa team that competed every night, and a Carolina team that quietly won every critical moment. That's the playoff template — not blowouts, just relentless execution under pressure.
What This Says
Carolina is built for deep playoff runs. The system is repeatable, the goaltending is elite, the discipline holds under chaos, the depth produces (Stankoven's line, Aho's clutch goals, Slavin eating PK minutes). This is the most complete team performance through one round in the bracket.
Round 2 Awaits
Carolina now waits for the winner of Philly–Pittsburgh, which could be decided as early as tonight. If Philly closes, it's a Metropolitan Division collision — two teams who handled their first round on system and structure, both currently looking like contenders, meeting next week.
Round 1 Verdict
Carolina was the most quietly dominant team of Round 1 — Colorado got more attention for the LA-style win, Philly for the 5–2 statement game. But neither played a series this clean. Four games, never trailing, eight-of-nine PKs in the clincher, four straight Andersen wins. They didn't just advance. They set a baseline.
Series 02 · West  ·  Survival

Dallas at Minnesota

Series Standing
Tied 2 — 2
  • Minnesota 3, Dallas 2 in overtime. Boldy tipped a Spurgeon shot at the left post with 29 seconds left in OT — a tap-in at the death, after two earlier disallowed Wild goals (one for goalie interference in regulation, one for a kicking motion in OT).
  • Dallas should have won this game. They were 2-for-2 on the power play and now sit 8-for-19 on special teams across the series. They got Robertson on the early PP and Heiskanen on the late one to take a 2–1 lead into the third.
  • Then Foligno tied it with 5:20 left on a gritty rebound — a Sturm shot, a fluky bounce off the end boards, Foligno tumbling over Oettinger's shoulder to poke it loose. Net-front, traffic, second effort — exactly what Dallas's structure is supposed to prevent.
  • The goaltending kept this game alive: Wallstedt 43 saves, Oettinger 40. The Wild are getting elite work from a 22-year-old in his playoff debut. The Stars are getting elite work from one of the best goalies in the league. Neither team can complain.
  • Critical context: Dallas played half the game with five defensemen after Lundkvist took a skate to the face on a tripping call. That fatigue showed up late. Lundkvist's status is unknown for Game 5.
  • The bigger pattern: Minnesota leads 9–4 at 5v5 in this series. Dallas owns special teams. The Wild own everything else. That's not a sustainable equation for either team — somebody has to crack first.
Dallas controls more of the play. Minnesota wins the moments. In a series this tight, that's the entire difference.
Dallas's Path
Get Lundkvist back. Six defensemen instead of five. Tighten 5v5 — they're getting outscored 9–4 there, which means the special teams edge is barely keeping them above water. Stop relying on the power play to save them. Game 5 at home is the chance to reassert.
Minnesota's Path
The power play has to start working — 1 goal in their last 15 chances. They're winning the series despite that, which is almost more damning. Wallstedt has been the wall. Stay disciplined. Win the 5v5 minutes. The formula works as long as it works.
Series Outlook · Updated
Tied 2–2 with Game 5 in Dallas Tuesday. This is now a true coin-flip series. Dallas should be the favorite based on home ice and special teams. But Minnesota is winning every category that doesn't involve a man advantage — and getting goaltending heroics. Six or seven games. The Wild's power play decides the outcome.
Series 03 · East  ·  Survival

Philadelphia at Pittsburgh

Series Standing
Flyers 3 — Penguins 1
  • Pittsburgh 4, Philadelphia 2. Sweep avoided. Crosby and Letang finally arrived — first goals of the series for both. After three games of nothing from Pittsburgh's stars, one game where they showed up was enough.
  • Crosby on the early power play, then a goaltending mistake gave the Pens a 2–0 cushion. For the first time in the series, Pittsburgh played with a lead instead of chasing one.
  • The coaching call: Arturs Silovs got the start. Same pattern we've seen with Buffalo (Lyon over Luukkonen) and Montreal (doubling down on Dach) — when something isn't working, the teams that change it win the next game.
  • Philly outshot Pittsburgh 30–21 and applied real pressure in the second. Cut it to 2–1. Then 3–2. Earlier in the series, this is where Pittsburgh would have collapsed. Tonight they held just enough.
  • Letang's third-period goal was the dagger — set up by a Crosby screen. The exact play Pittsburgh has been trying to manufacture for three games and finally got.
  • The honest read: Philly still leads 3–1. The structural problems aren't fixed — Pittsburgh got outshot, the power play remains inconsistent, the defensive wobbles are still there. They just had the kind of night that carries you to a Game 5.
That's enough to extend a series. Not yet enough to change it.
Pittsburgh's Path
Get the same Crosby and Letang back for Game 5 in Philly. If their stars need an extra night to load up, that's a 3–2 series. Stay with Silovs. Keep Crosby in front of the net for screens. Don't try to win pretty — they don't have it in them.
Philly's Path
Don't overreact. One bad night doesn't undo three good ones — they outshot Pittsburgh 30–21 even in defeat. Tighten the defensive coverage on Crosby. Get Vladar a clean game. Game 5 closes this if they bring the same structure that built the lead.
Series Outlook · Updated
Still Flyers in 5, probably. Pittsburgh extended a series they shouldn't survive on talent alone — but Philly's structural advantages haven't disappeared. The Pens need their stars to deliver in Game 5 too, not just once. The question isn't whether Pittsburgh can win one more game. It's whether they have two more in them.
§ 03

The patterns holding.

Round 1 is starting to produce verdicts, not just data points. Carolina's sweep validates everything we've said about the contenders' shared signature.

TREND / 01

The contenders share a signature.

  • Carolina: Andersen elite, structure airtight, depth scoring (Stankoven, Hall, Aho)
  • Colorado: System play, depth scoring (MacKinnon and Makar pointless, basically)
  • Philly: Vladar .947, fourth-line scoring, structure
  • None of these teams won Round 1 with stars. They won with system, goaltending, and depth. Round 2 will tell us if those traits scale.
TREND / 02

Special teams help — but they don't carry alone.

  • Dallas: 8-for-19 on special teams in their series. Tied 2–2. Outscored 9–4 at 5v5.
  • Buffalo: 0-for-35 on the power play. Up 2–1. Winning everywhere else.
  • Carolina: PK 8-for-9 in clincher. Won 5v5 too.
  • The teams winning are winning at 5v5 first. Special teams sharpens the edge. They don't replace it.
TREND / 03

Goaltending changes are series swings.

  • Buffalo: Lyon over Luukkonen → won Game 3
  • Montreal: Doubled down on a benched Dach → series lead
  • Pittsburgh: Silovs in for Game 4 → first win of the series
  • Trailing teams that change something win their next game more often than not. Three teams, three swaps, three wins. Not coincidence.
§ 04

What to watch next.

Two potential sweep-clinchers in the next 36 hours. The Round 2 bracket starts taking shape this weekend.

✓
Crosby in Game 5

Pittsburgh's stars showed up for one game. Tuesday in Philly is the test of whether that was a one-night trick or a real adjustment. If Crosby and Letang look like that twice, Philly suddenly has a series on its hands. If not, they don't.

✓
Colorado's Closer

Sunday in LA. The Avs are 3–0 with MacKinnon and Makar combining for two points. If they sweep without their stars even producing, the rest of the bracket should be terrified about what they look like awake.

✓
Round 2 Matchups Forming

Carolina is in. Their Round 2 opponent is the PHI/PIT winner — likely Philly. That's a Metropolitan Division series of two structurally elite teams. The collision between them might be the most-watched second-round series.

✓
Five Game 4s to Sort the Rest

BUF/BOS, DAL/MIN, MTL/TBL, UTA/VGK, ANA/EDM all play their Game 4s in the next 48–72 hours. Each one either tightens a 2–1 lead toward closing or reopens it. By midweek, the Round 2 picture will be 80% clear.

The Take
Carolina didn't sweep Ottawa. Ottawa was swept by an idea — that you win playoff hockey by winning the moments. Minnesota and Pittsburgh refused that idea tonight. Two teams that should have been closed out instead saved their seasons. The first Round 2 ticket is punched. The next ones aren't coming as easily as expected.

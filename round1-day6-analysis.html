§ 02

Two games, two upset trajectories.

Montreal won by inches in their third overtime. Utah won by miles in a momentum-avalanche game. Different shapes, same result — the favourites of Round 1 are getting smaller.

Series 05 · East  ·  Separation

Tampa at Montreal

Series Standing
Canadiens 2 — Lightning 1
  • Montreal 3, Tampa 2 in overtime. Lane Hutson wins it 2:09 into OT. Three games, three overtimes. The closest series in the tournament.
  • The story behind the box score: Kirby Dach. Blamed for Montreal's Game 2 OT loss. Social media wanted him benched. The coaching staff moved him to center on a new line — and that line drove both Habs goals.
  • Trust → deployment → production. That's a coaching win, not just a player win. A different staff plays the panic card. Montreal doubled down.
  • Vasilevskiy was excellent — 26 saves, denied Caufield and Anderson on third-period breakaways that should have ended it. Tampa had every chance.
  • Texier opened scoring at 4:53 of the first. Point answered on a Montreal power play. Hagel made it 2–1 in the second. Dach tied it from the top of the right circle through traffic. Hutson finishes it in OT.
  • Three OTs in a row says something fundamental about this matchup: no system advantage, no goaltending edge, no consistent gap. This is the series that will go seven.
Montreal didn't outplay Tampa. They made the right adjustment, won a few key moments, and capitalized when it mattered most. In a series this tight, that's all it takes.
Tampa's Path
Finish the breakaways. Vasilevskiy is doing his job at the other end — the top six is leaving goals on the table. Special teams need to deliver one big swing. The structural sameness means the team that converts one extra opportunity wins.
Montreal's Path
Keep Dach at center. Don't outsmart what's working. The home crowd is a real factor here — Bell Centre intensity has been a measurable swing in tight games. Win Game 4 at home and this becomes a 3–1 grip.
Series Outlook · Updated
Going seven. Probably. This isn't separation hockey — it's coin-flip hockey, and Montreal has won two of three coin flips. The Hutson goal was a sliver. The Dach line move was a sliver. The next three games will be decided by slivers too. Whoever wins more of them wins the series.
Series 07 · West  ·  Flipped

Vegas at Utah

Series Standing
Mammoth 2 — Golden Knights 1
  • Utah 4, Vegas 2. The 4–2 lies. This was 4–0 midway through the game, with both Vegas goals coming late when the result was settled.
  • Vegas started strong. Possession, early chances, the kind of opening minutes that should produce a 1–0 lead. Couldn't beat the goalie. Then conceded a weird goal. That sequence is the entire game.
  • Utah added a power-play goal, then two more early in the second. Competitive game to 4–0 blowout in roughly twenty minutes. Classic playoff snowball: missed chances → confidence drains → opponent scores → structure breaks → spiral.
  • Vegas down 4–0 had possession, power plays, third-period push. Generated nothing sustained, nothing high-danger. A 4–0 hole against a structured team is functionally the game.
  • Utah's read: clinical, not dominant. Won every critical moment without controlling whole stretches. Special teams, turnovers, momentum swings — all converted.
  • This is a different shape of game than anything else this round. Not LA/COL margins. Not MTL/TBL coin flips. This was a momentum avalanche — and Utah's young core is built to ride those.
Utah didn't dominate the game. They punished every mistake and rode the momentum perfectly. In playoff hockey, that's often all it takes.
Vegas's Path
Finish the early chances. The process wasn't broken — execution was. Tighten the defensive coverage on rebounds and weird bounces. The bigger question: how does this team handle being chased? They've struggled with momentum swings all series. Game 4 is must-win or this becomes a 3–1 hole on the road.
Utah's Path
Keep playing reactive, opportunistic hockey. Don't try to dominate — Utah's identity is speed and second efforts, not territorial control. Cooley and Guenther stay hot. Crash the net. The series is on Utah's terms now, not Vegas's.
Series Outlook · Updated
After Game 1 we said Vegas showed they could close. After Game 2 we said the upset watch was real. After Game 3, Utah is the upset. Vegas is still in this — but they have to win three of the next four against a team that just blew their doors off on its own ice. The Mammoth aren't a Cinderella anymore. They're a problem.
§ 03

The bracket has split.

Day 6 is when one story ends and another begins. The closing series and the coin-flip series are running on different physics now.

TREND / 01

The bracket has bifurcated.

  • Three series at 3–0 (PHI, CAR, COL). All facing potential sweeps in Game 4.
  • Four series in firm control (DAL, BUF, MTL, UTA — all 2–1).
  • One series live and tilting (ANA leading EDM late).
  • By morning, every Round 1 series will have a leader. No more even matchups. The bracket is fully resolving.
TREND / 02

Coaching calls are series calls.

  • Buffalo: Lyon over Luukkonen. Won Game 3. Goaltending solved.
  • Montreal: Doubled down on Dach. Dach drives Game 3. Series lead.
  • Anaheim: Promoted Gauthier to the top line. Game-winner in Game 2.
  • The teams trusting their internal calls are winning. The teams second-guessing are not.
TREND / 03

Round 1 has four game-shapes now.

  • Strangleholds — PHI, CAR, COL. Built on structure and depth.
  • Coin flips — MTL/TBL. Three games, three OTs. Decided by slivers.
  • Margin games — DAL/MIN. Special teams flip the result.
  • Avalanches — UTA over VGK. Miss → spiral → 4–0 → over.
  • Four ways to win a playoff game. Round 1 has shown them all.
§ 04

What to watch tonight.

Two Game 3s in the last even series. Both could push the bracket from "three coin flips" to "one coin flip" by morning.

✓
Vegas in a Hole

Down 2–1 on Utah's ice with another road game coming. The Knights have struggled with momentum swings all series. Game 4 is a must-win — lose it and they're staring at a 3–1 deficit nobody saw coming a week ago.

✓
Edmonton's Last Five Minutes

Down 5–4 in the third, Edmonton has clawed back into a game they were getting blown out of. This finish decides everything. A late equalizer or winner saves their season. A loss puts them down 2–1 on home ice with McDavid still hunting his first series point.

✓
Bell Centre Effect

Montreal's home atmosphere has been a measurable factor in Games 1 and 3. Game 4 still in Montreal. If the Habs feed off the crowd one more time, this series is functionally over before they go back to Tampa.

✓
The Three Sweeps

Philly, Carolina, and Colorado all up 3–0. Each could close Game 4 in the next 24–48 hours. Three sweeps in Round 1 would be a near-historic result and would reshape the schedule for everyone.

The Take
Day 6 is the day Round 1 stopped being one tournament and became two. The favourites are running. The underdogs are no longer hanging on — they're leading. Utah, Anaheim, Montreal. Three series the favourites were supposed to control. Three series the favourites don't.

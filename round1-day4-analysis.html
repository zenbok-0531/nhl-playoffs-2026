Game 3 is when the shape of a series becomes undeniable. Philadelphia walked into Pittsburgh down one early, then scored three goals in six minutes, then another, then another. A 5–2 final that felt like a 7–1. The Penguins have now scored four goals in three games. The Flyers have Dan Vladar, a depth chart that's outworking a star chart, and — after a 2-for-3 night — a power play that's finally earning its keep. This isn't a series anymore. It's a countdown.

§ 02

Philly–Pittsburgh, Game 3.

Six minutes of hockey erased a series. Pittsburgh is out of answers — and running out of games.

Series 03 · East  ·  Stranglehold

Philadelphia at Pittsburgh

Series Standing
Flyers 3 — Penguins 0
  • Philadelphia 5, Pittsburgh 2. The margin lies. This wasn't close.
  • Malkin scored first on the power play. It looked briefly like this was Pittsburgh's night to flip the series.
  • Then a massive scrum. Eleven minor penalties at once. A Flyers power play out of the chaos. Zegras scored. Everything changed.
  • Within six minutes, the Flyers scored three goals — Zegras, Ristolainen, Seeler. Game over. Series effectively over.
  • Karlsson cut it to 3–2 to give Pittsburgh a pulse. Noah Cates killed it inside a minute. Then Tippett on the empty net.
  • Pittsburgh's series totals: four goals in three games. One even-strength goal. That's not variance. That's systemic.
  • Vladar in the series: .947 save percentage. Not heroic — just quietly closing the door every time Pittsburgh remembers how to shoot.
Pittsburgh had a moment. Philadelphia overwhelmed them with structure and a scoring burst. The series is effectively over.
Pittsburgh's Path
Generate interior offense — period. Four goals in three games is the entire problem, not a symptom. Simplify. Fewer east-west plays, more pucks to the net, more bodies in front. Hope for a goaltending swing. Not much else left.
Philly's Path
Don't change anything. Play to close. Same forecheck, same net-front pressure, same trust in Vladar. Game 4 is about execution, not adjustment.
Series Outlook · Updated
Flyers in 4 or 5. The only remaining question is whether Pittsburgh can score more than one or two goals in a single game. If they can't — and so far they haven't — this ends on home ice.
§ 03

Trends hardening.

Three games deep into a playoff series is when the patterns stop being patterns and start being verdicts.

TREND / 01

The 2–0 leads are holding.

  • Philly went up 3–0. Carolina and Colorado both sit 2–0 and look in control.
  • No team has ever come back from 3–0. Only four have come back from 2–0 in the last decade.
  • The bracket is narrowing fast. Three of eight series already have a team on the ropes.
TREND / 02

Scoring bursts decide games.

  • Philly: 3 goals in 6 minutes tonight
  • Boston: 3 goals in the 2nd period to flip Game 2
  • Buffalo: 4 goals in 7 minutes to steal Game 1
  • Playoff games don't trickle. They flip in short, decisive windows.
TREND / 03

Pittsburgh has collapsed.

  • Four goals in three games. One even-strength goal.
  • Power play 0-for-7 until tonight's Malkin goal. Shorthanded goal against in Game 2.
  • This is the first series-level collapse of Round 1. And it happened to a team that was supposed to be competitive.
§ 04

What to watch next.

Two games tonight. Both could reshape their series by morning.

✓
Dallas at Minnesota, Game 3

The real swing series of Round 1. Tied 1–1 and now on Wild ice. If Minnesota wins this, the upset narrative is back on. If Dallas takes it, the Stars are in control of a series they were "supposed" to win.

✓
Anaheim at Edmonton, Game 2

McDavid held pointless in Game 1 and the Oilers still won. That's not a number he'll accept twice. Watch for a statement night — or an Anaheim upset building.

✓
Penguins Coaching Response

Down 3–0, offense nonexistent, goaltending shaky. Line combinations haven't worked. At some point the coaching staff has to try something different. Game 4 tells us what.

✓
The Next Stranglehold

Carolina and Colorado both enter Game 3 up 2–0 tomorrow. If either takes a 3–0 lead, three of eight series will be functionally over before the weekend.

The Take
Game 3 is where hockey stops pretending. Philadelphia isn't trying to win a series — they're trying to finish one. Pittsburgh isn't losing. They're being put away.

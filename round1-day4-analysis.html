The Separation.

Game 3s are where the pretenders and the contenders start separating. Philadelphia drew the sharpest line yet — a 3–0 stranglehold on Pittsburgh. But up north, Anaheim walked into Edmonton and won a 6–4 chaos game, tying the series and putting a real question mark on the Oilers. One more Game 3 tonight decides the day.

Game 3 is when the shape of a series becomes undeniable. Philadelphia walked into Pittsburgh down one early, then scored three goals in six minutes, then another, then another. A 5–2 final that felt like a 7–1. Meanwhile, Anaheim walked into Edmonton, traded chaos for chaos, and won a 6–4 track meet that held nothing of the Oilers' usual advantages. The Penguins can't score. McDavid can't either. Two very different reasons favourites are in trouble — one structural, one impossible to ignore.

§ 02

Two games, two favourites in trouble.

Philadelphia tightened a stranglehold. Anaheim cracked Edmonton wide open. Different pressure, same direction.

Series 03 · East  ·  Stranglehold

Philadelphia at Pittsburgh

Series Standing
Flyers 3 — Penguins 0
  • Philadelphia 5, Pittsburgh 2. The margin lies. This wasn't close.
  • Malkin scored first on the power play. It looked briefly like this was Pittsburgh's night to flip the series.
  • Then a massive scrum. Eleven minor penalties at once. A Flyers power play out of the chaos. Zegras scored. Everything changed.
  • Within six minutes, the Flyers scored three goals — Zegras, Ristolainen, Seeler. Game over. Series effectively over.
  • Karlsson cut it to 3–2 to give Pittsburgh a pulse. Noah Cates killed it inside a minute. Then Tippett on the empty net.
  • Pittsburgh's series totals: four goals in three games. One even-strength goal. That's not variance. That's systemic.
  • Vladar in the series: .947 save percentage. Not heroic — just quietly closing the door every time Pittsburgh remembers how to shoot.
Pittsburgh had a moment. Philadelphia overwhelmed them with structure and a scoring burst. The series is effectively over.
Pittsburgh's Path
Generate interior offense — period. Four goals in three games is the entire problem, not a symptom. Simplify. Fewer east-west plays, more pucks to the net, more bodies in front. Hope for a goaltending swing. Not much else left.
Philly's Path
Don't change anything. Play to close. Same forecheck, same net-front pressure, same trust in Vladar. Game 4 is about execution, not adjustment.
Series Outlook · Updated
Flyers in 4 or 5. The only remaining question is whether Pittsburgh can score more than one or two goals in a single game. If they can't — and so far they haven't — this ends on home ice.
Series 08 · West  ·  Flipped

Anaheim at Edmonton

Series Standing
Ducks 1 — Oilers 1
  • Anaheim 6, Edmonton 4. Connor McDavid is now pointless through two playoff games. That's the series.
  • Pure chaos from puck drop. Multiple lead changes. Ducks led 3–1, then 4–2. Oilers tied it 4–4 late in the third.
  • Special teams ran the game — and Anaheim won both sides. Two power-play goals plus a shorthanded goal off a McDavid turnover. In a playoff game that math is fatal.
  • Cutter Gauthier got promoted to the top line. Scored twice, including the game-winner with five minutes left. Empty-netter sealed it.
  • McDavid: zero points, brief injury scare, the turnover that led to Anaheim's shorthanded goal. Edmonton's engine isn't driving the car.
  • Draisaitl's line was productive again. The rest of the lineup was not. One line cannot carry a playoff series.
Edmonton had chances. Anaheim won the chaos, the special teams battle, and the final moments. That's why the series is tied.
Edmonton's Path
Free up McDavid. The Ducks are pressuring him hard and Edmonton hasn't answered. Tighten defensive coverage — four goals against in a home Game 2 is not playoff hockey. Fix the penalty kill. Find a second line that contributes.
Anaheim's Path
Keep the pressure on McDavid. Keep winning special teams. Lean into the chaos — the Ducks are the team that thrives when this series stays high-event, and they know it.
Series Outlook · Updated
After Game 1 we said Edmonton was still the favourite but not comfortable. After Game 2, they're in genuine trouble. If McDavid doesn't emerge in Game 3, Anaheim isn't just stealing a game — they're winning a series they weren't supposed to win.
§ 03

Trends hardening.

Three games deep into a playoff series is when the patterns stop being patterns and start being verdicts.

TREND / 01

The favourites are buckling.

  • Pittsburgh: 4 goals in 3 games. Down 3–0.
  • Edmonton: Surrendered 6 goals at home. McDavid pointless through 2.
  • Buffalo, Tampa, Vegas — all 1–1 after Game 1 control evaporated
  • The road team has won six of the last eight games across the bracket. Home ice is not a shield this spring.
TREND / 02

Scoring bursts decide games.

  • Philly: 3 goals in 6 minutes tonight to bury Pittsburgh
  • Anaheim: Two 2-goal leads on Edmonton, never gave up tempo
  • Boston: 3 goals in the 2nd period to flip their Game 2
  • Playoff games don't trickle. They flip in short, decisive windows.
TREND / 03

Stars that can't score become liabilities.

  • McDavid: 0 points in 2, plus the turnover that led to Anaheim's shorthanded goal
  • Keller: quiet through two for Utah's Day 1 effort
  • Crosby & Malkin: not enough, not often enough, not at 5v5
  • In tight games, a silent superstar costs their team more than a silent role player. The minutes are there. The production isn't.
§ 04

What to watch next.

Two games tonight. Both could reshape their series by morning.

✓
Dallas at Minnesota, Game 3

The real swing series of Round 1. Tied 1–1 and now on Wild ice. If Minnesota wins this, the upset narrative is back on. If Dallas takes it, the Stars are in control of a series they were "supposed" to win.

✓
McDavid's Game 3

Zero points in two games. An icon doesn't stay silent for three. The question isn't whether he finds his game — it's whether Edmonton's role players help him once he does.

✓
Penguins Coaching Response

Down 3–0, offense nonexistent, goaltending shaky. Line combinations haven't worked. At some point the coaching staff has to try something different. Game 4 tells us what.

✓
The Next Stranglehold

Carolina and Colorado both enter Game 3 up 2–0 tomorrow. If either takes a 3–0 lead, three of eight series will be functionally over before the weekend.

The Take
Game 3 is where hockey stops pretending. Pittsburgh isn't losing — they're being put away. Edmonton isn't struggling — they're being exposed. When favourites look this unconvincing, the upsets stop feeling like upsets.

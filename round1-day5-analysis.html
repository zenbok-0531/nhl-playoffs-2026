§ 02

Two games, two formulas.

Buffalo changed what wasn't working. Carolina kept doing what was. Both paths led to series control.

Series 06 · East  ·  Separation

Buffalo at Boston

Series Standing
Sabres 2 — Bruins 1
  • Buffalo 3, Boston 1. A game decided by a goaltending call and a missed shot — two moments that redefined a series.
  • Alex Lyon got the start over Luukkonen. We flagged Sabres goaltending as the open question on Day 3. Today it got its answer: calm, composed, sharp under pressure, elite in the slot.
  • Boston scored first (third straight game — strange trend for a team leading the series). The moment that defined the night came minutes later.
  • Arvidsson missed a penalty shot. Could have been 2–0 Boston, could have been the dagger. Instead: Byram ties it inside one shift. Tuch scores the winner from a prime slot chance. Östlund — rookie, playoff debut — gets the primary assist on the tying goal and looks veteran doing it.
  • Buffalo's power play is still 0-for-its-last-35. Four penalties taken. Allowed the penalty shot. And they still won.
  • Boston outplayed Buffalo on structure. Buffalo out-executed Boston in the moments that mattered. That's the whole difference.
Buffalo didn't dominate this game. They out-executed Boston in the moments that mattered most. That is often the difference in playoff hockey.
Buffalo's Path
Stay with Lyon — he earned it. Fix the power play before it becomes the series-ending story. Keep leaning on depth: Tuch as veteran finisher, Byram driving the blue line, Östlund already looking like a real playoff piece.
Boston's Path
Finish the chances. A missed penalty shot doesn't happen twice, but not converting 2–0 leads does. Get the forecheck going earlier. Demand more from top-six players — Arvidsson can't be the guy who gets the penalty shot and misses it.
Series Outlook · Updated
Buffalo now has what it didn't have in Game 1: a goaltender they trust, a formula that survives imperfection, and a series lead. Boston has to win the next game or this becomes a 3–1 hole. Heavy pressure flips to the team that was supposed to be the underdog here.
Series 01 · East  ·  Stranglehold

Ottawa at Carolina

Series Standing
Hurricanes 3 — Senators 0
  • Carolina 2, Ottawa 1. The scoreline lies harder than any other this round.
  • Carolina controlled pace from puck drop. Ottawa was supposed to bring the physicality and push back. Instead: Carolina dictated tempo, flow, and every swing of the game.
  • Stankoven opened scoring again — third straight game he's gotten Carolina on the board first. Pattern, not coincidence.
  • Ottawa's equalizer came off a Carolina turnover (Batherson). The only way Ottawa has generated offense this series is Carolina making a mistake.
  • Carolina's response: under three minutes. Clean sequence, Miller to Blake, back in front. No chaos, no fluke, no panic.
  • This is the tell: Carolina isn't winning with goaltending theft or power-play bursts. It's system-driven, repeatable, structurally sound. The profile of a team built for a deep run.
  • The deeper read: Ottawa isn't being shut down — they're being out-executed in the moments that decide games. Chances exist. Finishes don't.
Carolina isn't just beating Ottawa. They're controlling the conditions of the game itself — tempo, momentum, execution windows.
Ottawa's Path
Honest answer: there isn't one that turns this series around. The Senators need sustained pressure, not moments — and the structural tools to do it haven't shown up. Best they can do is avoid the sweep by stealing a chaotic game at home.
Carolina's Path
Don't change anything. Close it out. Same opening pace, same Stankoven first line, same trust in Andersen. Ottawa's confidence is eroded, the injuries are stacking — press the advantage.
Series Outlook · Updated
Functionally over. Canes in 4. And here's the scary part for the rest of the bracket: Carolina is doing this while losing faceoffs badly (~60% Ottawa), getting minimal offense from defense, and no points from Jarvis. They have room to get better. That's the profile of the scariest team left in Round 1.
§ 03

Trends hardening.

Day 5 is where patterns turn into verdicts. Five series now have a team in control — and they share a signature.

TREND / 01

Goaltending calls are series calls.

  • Buffalo swapped Luukkonen for Lyon. Won Game 3.
  • Carolina's Andersen flipped from a shaky regular season to elite playoff form — top in goals saved above expected
  • Philly's Vladar: .947 SV% across the series, all three wins.
  • Colorado's Wedgewood: penalty-shot save in Game 2. Won in OT.
  • The five teams in firm control all have goalies playing above expectation. Not volume saves — timing saves.
TREND / 02

Execution beats dominance.

  • Buffalo was outplayed tonight. Won anyway.
  • Ottawa won 60% of faceoffs tonight. Lost 2–1.
  • Minnesota dominated stretches of Game 3. Lost in 2OT.
  • Pittsburgh doubled Philly in shot attempts earlier. Lost 3–0.
  • Territorial edges, faceoff wins, shot attempts — none of it matters without finish. The teams in control all share the same trait: converting when it counts.
TREND / 03

The sweep watch is live.

  • Philly 3–0 on Pittsburgh. Could sweep Game 4.
  • Carolina 3–0 on Ottawa. Could sweep Game 4.
  • Colorado 2–0 on LA, Game 3 tonight. Third stranglehold in play.
  • Two of eight series functionally decided. A third possible tonight. The bracket is closing faster than expected.
§ 04

What to watch tonight.

Two Game 3s with three possible outcomes each. The next 24 hours decide how fast the bracket closes.

✓
Carolina's Ceiling

3–0 with Jarvis pointless, 60% faceoff losses, and minimal blue-line offense. That's a team with room to get better. Watch how Game 4 looks with a sweep on the table — if they play it clean, this team becomes everyone's problem.

✓
LA's 5v5 Puzzle

Every LA goal in the series has come on special teams. You cannot beat Colorado on that alone. Game 3 is the deadline — either they generate 5v5 offense tonight or they're down 3–0 by morning.

✓
Boston's Response Window

Down 2–1 heading into Game 4. The Bruins are structurally sound but losing games they feel they should win. If they can't find a converting touch in Game 4, they're the next team in real trouble.

✓
Buffalo's Power Play

0-for-35. They're winning the series anyway, which is almost more damning — imagine if it worked. Boston will draw more penalties. Eventually the drought ends or it doesn't.

The Take
Day 5 is where the teams that know themselves start winning games they should. Buffalo found a formula in Lyon. Carolina has been running theirs from game one. One is a comeback story. The other is starting to look like a Cup story.

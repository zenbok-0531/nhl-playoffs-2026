Day 11 is when Round 1 became a learning competition. Montreal lost Game 4 by failing to manage a momentum swing — Tampa scored three unanswered after a Crozier hit and took back home ice. Three days later, Montreal got the same shape of game: 2–0 lead, Tampa pushing back. This time the Habs held. They tightened their special-teams discipline, kept Tampa from creating the emotional turning point, and walked out of Bell Centre with a 3–2 series lead. The young team that lost Game 4 didn't show up in Game 5. The team that played in Game 5 had learned.

§ 02

Montreal flips it back.

Same shape of game as Game 4. Different result. Montreal didn't win Game 5 with a new tactic — they won it by not making the same mistake twice.

Series 05 · East  ·  Flipped

Tampa at Montreal

Series Standing
Habs 3 — Lightning 2
  • Montreal 3, Tampa 2. The Habs flipped the series back to 3–2 in their favour with a road win at Bell Centre — Game 6 now goes Friday in Tampa, with Tampa needing to win at home to force a Game 7.
  • The shape of Game 5 mirrored Game 4 — Montreal up 2–0, Tampa pushing back, late physicality. Game 4 ended in a Tampa rally and a 3–2 Lightning win. Game 5 ended in Montreal closing it out. Same setup, different ending. The variable was Montreal's discipline and execution under pressure.
  • Cole Caufield finally on the scoresheet after 51 regular-season goals and a quiet series — albeit on the power play, not at 5v5. Suzuki's line still hasn't broken through at even strength, but the late-period PP goal was a real psychological release.
  • Tampa's adjustment formula — Cooper's mid-series lineup tweaks, the Hagel-Kucherov-Point reunion, the Cirelli-Gourde shutdown line — had carried them through Games 2 and 4. Tonight none of it activated cleanly. Hagel was still dangerous but didn't get the deflection or rebound that swings games.
  • The Bell Centre crowd did its work again. Montreal is now 3–0 in this series at home, with the Bell Centre delivering exactly the energy lift the Habs need on home ice. Game 7 — if there is one — would also be in Montreal.
  • The Athletic-flagged structural problem hasn't disappeared: the Suzuki line still hasn't scored at 5v5 in five games. Montreal is winning a series with their top line dormant at even strength. That's either the most surprising contender story of Round 1, or a time bomb waiting for Round 2.
We didn't play a good enough third — they're a veteran team. They're very good at drawing penalties. — Martin St. Louis, after Game 4
What Changed
Discipline. Game 4: Montreal took penalties at the wrong moments and Tampa converted. Game 5: penalty count down, momentum swings managed, late-game lead protected. That's the kind of in-series correction that separates teams that go home from teams that advance.
Montreal's Path
Game 6 in Tampa with a chance to advance. Cooper now has last-change advantage, the most likely outcome is Tampa pushing this back to a Game 7 in Montreal. The Habs' real test is whether they can avoid Game 4's mistake on the road, in a building they don't control.
Tampa's Path
Hagel needs the deflection or rebound that's been missing. Vasilevskiy needs a swing performance. Cooper needs the matchups to work the way they did in Game 4. Most importantly: find the Crozier moment again. Tampa won Game 4 by changing the game's emotional shape with a single hit. They need that energy to reappear in Game 6.
Series Outlook · Updated
Habs in 7, slight lean — assuming the series goes the distance, which now looks likely. Montreal proved they can play both styles of hockey: the loose, momentum-driven version they won Games 1–3 with, and the disciplined, structured version they won Game 5 with. Tampa's veteran adjustment edge isn't gone, but it's not decisive anymore. This series is now a coin flip in everything but the score.
§ 03

The patterns refining.

The "trailing teams win Game 5" trend held for a fifth straight series. The "young teams break under pressure" trend got challenged. Round 1's teaching moments are accumulating.

TREND / 01

The trailing-team Game 5 streak hit five.

  • Pittsburgh (down 3–1) → won Game 5
  • Boston (down 3–1) → won Game 5 OT
  • Edmonton (down 3–1) → won Game 5
  • Minnesota (tied 2–2) → won Game 5 to take lead
  • Montreal (tied 2–2) → won Game 5 to take lead
  • Five Game 5s, five trailing teams won. Desperation outworking expectation, repeatedly.
TREND / 02

Adaptation beats raw talent right now.

  • Buffalo: Lyon swap → 3-game win streak
  • Edmonton: complete game discipline → Game 5 win
  • Pittsburgh: simplified offense → second-life series
  • Montreal: penalty discipline → series lead
  • The teams making in-series corrections are advancing past teams that arrived perfect.
TREND / 03

Bell Centre is a real factor.

  • Montreal 3–0 at home in this series
  • Game 7 (if needed) would be in Montreal
  • The crowd has produced measurable game-state energy in 3 of 5 games
  • Home ice in playoff hockey usually evens out. The Bell Centre this series has been a genuine variable.

The Take
Round 1 is over for two teams and approaching its endgame for six. The teams advancing right now aren't the most talented. They're the ones learning fastest. Carolina learned how to absorb chaos before Round 1 started. Colorado learned how to win without their stars. Now Montreal, Edmonton, and Pittsburgh are learning in real time — adapting between games in ways that can change a series in 48 hours. The bracket is teaching its own lesson, and the favourites still standing are the ones taking notes.

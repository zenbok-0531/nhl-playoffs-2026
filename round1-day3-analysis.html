Day 3 is when the adjustments start showing up — all at once. Tampa matched Montreal's physicality and won in overtime. Boston exploited Buffalo's goaltending and ran away with the second period. Utah answered Vegas's Game 1 closer with its own late-game closer, scoring on a rebound with six minutes left to even the series. And Colorado — the only favourite not tested — showed why they're favourites. Absorbed LA's late go-ahead goal, tied it inside three minutes, won it at the crease in overtime. Four games, three series flipped, one series tightened. The tournament's shape is clearer by the hour.

§ 02

Four Game 2s, four different stories.

Physical adjustment. Goaltending exploitation. Speed breakthrough. Patient survival. Four different templates for winning a playoff game.

Series 05 · East  ·  Reversed

Montreal at Tampa Bay

Series Standing
Canadiens 1 — Lightning 1
  • Tampa 3, Montreal 2 — in overtime. The adjustment worked. Tampa dressed a heavier lineup, matched Montreal's physicality from puck drop, and waited for one mistake.
  • Thirty penalty minutes in the first period alone. Scrums, fights (Slafkovsky dropped the gloves), after-whistle chaos. This wasn't a skill game — it was a war.
  • Montreal actually took control. 2–1 lead late in the third, Josh Anderson wrecking people, Hutson scoring on the power play.
  • Then the turn. Slafkovsky turnover on a clearing attempt. Kucherov made him pay. Tied game — 40 minutes of Montreal work erased in one shift.
  • The missed chance: late Montreal power play after an Anderson hit-from-behind. Zero goals. In playoffs, that's the window you don't get back.
  • OT: Moser walks a puck and beats Dobes clean. Tampa made the play. Montreal didn't. That's the whole playoff margin right there.
Montreal controlled stretches. Tampa capitalized on the one moment that mattered. That's why the series is tied.
Montreal's Path
Clean up the puck management — especially D-zone exits. You can't give elite stars second chances. Finish the power play when it comes late. Keep the physical edge, lose the volatility — the chaos is hurting them as much as Tampa.
Tampa's Path
Keep the heavier lineup. The physical matchup is no longer lopsided. Continue letting Montreal take chances and feed Kucherov the leftovers — the series is tied precisely because Tampa stopped trying to outskate and started trying to outlast.
Series Outlook · Updated
After Game 1 we said Tampa was a clear favourite and the series was open. After Game 2, it's a true toss-up. Montreal can dictate play — but until they stop handing Kucherov free goals, they can't win four of these. This is a long series now.
Series 06 · East  ·  Reversed

Boston at Buffalo

Series Standing
Sabres 1 — Bruins 1
  • Boston 4, Buffalo 2. The Day 1 thesis just got rewritten. The Bruins we said "can't play anything but goalie-dependent hockey" came in and scored four.
  • Buffalo started strong. Generated pressure, controlled chances early. Then the second period happened.
  • Three Boston goals in the second — an Arvidsson 5-hole beat, a soft dump-in goal, a Zacha power-play redirect. This wasn't dominance. It was exploitation.
  • Arvidsson again, 16 seconds into the third. Luukkonen pulled immediately. Game effectively over before most of the crowd sat down.
  • Buffalo pushed late (Byram, Krebs) to make it 4–2, but by then it was scoreboard management, not a real comeback window
  • Sabres power play now 0-for-9 in the series. 0-for-31 dating back. This is a structural problem, not variance.
  • Faceoffs: 18–2 Boston in the first period. That's how you dictate territory without dominating possession.
Buffalo played well early. Boston capitalized on a short window of weakness. That's all it took to flip the game and the series momentum.
Buffalo's Path
Make the goaltending decision. Luukkonen's rhythm is gone; the soft goals aren't going to stop themselves. Fix the power play or stop taking them — 0-for-9 is actively losing. Emotional discipline: Thompson off the ice during the comeback push is a self-inflicted wound.
Boston's Path
Keep pressuring the goaltending — every shot matters against a netminder this shaky. Win the faceoff battle again and the possession follows. The depth finally showed up (Arvidsson, Zacha); keep leaning on it instead of waiting for top-line magic.
Series Outlook · Updated
After Game 1 we said Buffalo was firmly in control. After Game 2, the series is reset — and momentum is with Boston. The question isn't whether Buffalo can still win it; it's whether they can stabilize goaltending and special teams before this becomes a trend.
Series 07 · West  ·  Reversed

Utah at Vegas

Series Standing
Mammoth 1 — Golden Knights 1
  • Utah 3, Vegas 2. The Day 1 "Close" just got closed on. Vegas won Game 1 by wearing Utah down. Utah won Game 2 by never letting Vegas settle.
  • Back-and-forth chess match. Goals traded in each of the first two periods — this game was always heading to a one-goal finish.
  • Chaos scoring on both sides. A Vegas goal deflected off a defender's skate. A Utah goal went in off two Vegas defenders. Clean hockey this wasn't.
  • The flow shift: Vegas looked comfortable in period one, then lost the middle frame entirely. Dylan Guenther driving. Utah's speed broke Vegas's structure.
  • Third period tied 2–2. Vegas pushed early. Couldn't finish. Then Logan Cooley scored on a rebound with six minutes left — point shot, chaos, net-front finish. Playoff OT offence played in regulation time.
  • Two-game playoff career, two goals for Cooley. Star emergence in real time.
Vegas played well. Utah's speed and ability to win second chances made the difference when it mattered most.
Utah's Path
Lean harder into speed. The rush chances are where this team lives — force Vegas to defend in motion. Crash the net. Cooley's going to keep scoring if they keep creating rebounds in front.
Vegas's Path
Control the neutral zone better — Utah is winning transition, not possession. Clean up crease coverage; second-chance goals are the story. The bad bounces will regress, but the structural leaks are real.
Series Outlook · Updated
After Game 1 we said Vegas showed they can close. After Game 2, Utah showed they can open. Series fully reset — and momentum is with the younger, faster team. The upset watch is real again.
Series 04 · West  ·  Confirmed

LA Kings at Colorado

Series Standing
Avalanche 2 — Kings 0
  • Colorado 2, LA 1 — in overtime. The favourite just showed what patience looks like.
  • Colorado controlled 5v5 all night. Forecheck heavy, shot advantage early (14–6 in the first), O-zone time stacked. But Forsberg was standing on his head — 34 saves including a penalty shot stop.
  • Chaos first period: big hits, scrums, seven minor penalties. A grind game where momentum kept getting interrupted — not a flow game, and that favoured the structured team.
  • Panarin scored late in the third to make it 1–0 Kings. In a low-event game, that usually is the dagger. It wasn't.
  • Landeskog answered three minutes later. No panic. No overreaction. Just execution. That's what elite teams do.
  • OT: Nicolas Roy buries a rebound at the crease. Playoff OT goals live in front of the net. Always have. Always will.
There's no bicycles allowed on the highway.
Laughton's Line
The quote of the playoffs so far, from Scott Laughton after getting flattened between whistles and pulled for a TV break. Translation: keep your head up. A good sport, taking the hit and finding the line. The kind of moment that only playoff hockey produces.
LA's Path
Generate 5v5 offense. Both LA goals in the series have come on special teams — that's unsustainable. Forsberg is doing his job. The skaters need to create traffic and finish chances inside, not perimeter.
Colorado's Path
Keep doing exactly what they're doing. Low-risk, disciplined, net-front-attack hockey. Trust the process over results. The results are coming.
Series Outlook · Updated
We said Colorado was the strong favourite after Game 1. After Game 2, they're firmly in control and playing the kind of hockey that wins Cups. Avs in 5 unless LA suddenly solves the 5v5 puzzle.
§ 03

The pattern emerging.

Seven series tested. Four reversed. Three confirmed. The tournament's shape is starting to look inevitable.

TREND / 01

Day 1 reads rarely survive Game 2.

  • Seven Day 1 series tested. Four reversals, three confirmations.
  • Every upset (MIN, MTL, BUF) got answered. The "Close" too (VGK flipped by UTA).
  • The survivors — Philly, Carolina, Colorado — all won on structure + patience. The confirmations share a signature, not a score.
TREND / 02

Special teams are the sharpest edge.

  • Buffalo's PP: 0-for-9 this series. 0-for-31 dating back.
  • Pittsburgh: 0-for-7. Dallas: 1-for-1 to beat Minnesota.
  • LA's entire series offense has come on special teams — which is precisely why Colorado is up 2–0.
  • Every close series is being decided here first.
TREND / 03

Elite teams absorb and answer.

  • Colorado: gave up a late go-ahead goal, tied it in three minutes, won in OT
  • Carolina: survived a 6-on-4 power play and a double-OT to hold serve
  • Philly: let Pittsburgh dominate shot attempts 75–38, won 3–0
  • The favourites still standing at 2–0 all share this trait — they don't panic when the other team has its moment. They wait for theirs.
§ 04

What to watch next.

Three Game 2s coming in 48 hours. Every one has an upset watch attached.

✓
Slafkovsky's Response

Hat trick in Game 1, game-losing turnover in Game 2. Young players often follow exactly that arc. Is Game 3 another superstar night, or does the mistake get into his head?

✓
Sabres Goaltending

Luukkonen got pulled. The backup is unproven. Buffalo needs to make a call — and whichever goalie gets Game 3 will need to answer a question the team hasn't had to ask in months.

✓
Cooley's Third Act

Two playoff games, two goals — one of them a Game 2 winner. Utah's young core is arriving. Watch whether Cooley's confidence turns into a real Game 3 takeover.

✓
LA's 5v5 Puzzle

Every LA goal this series has come on special teams. You cannot win a series like that against Colorado. Game 3 tells us whether they have a Plan B — or whether they're cooked.

The Take
Day 1 told us what might be true. Day 2 tested it. Day 3 closed the Day 1 ledger — four reversals, three confirmations. The upsets don't hold, but the favourites that do hold share one trait: they absorb the other team's best moment and answer with their own.

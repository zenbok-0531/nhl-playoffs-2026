§ 02

The Game 2s, a deeper read.

Two games. Two favourites. One shared lesson — you close playoff games by taking them away, not by winning them.

Series 03 · East  ·  Confirmed

Philadelphia at Pittsburgh

Series Standing
Flyers 2 — Penguins 0
  • Final: Philadelphia 3, Pittsburgh 0. Shot attempts: Pittsburgh 75, Philly 38. Those numbers don't contradict — they explain each other.
  • Pittsburgh had the puck. Philly had the ice. Neutral zone clogged, blue line stacked, everything funnelled to the perimeter.
  • The Penguins power play is now 0-for-7 in the playoffs — and gave up a shorthanded goal. That's not neutral. That's actively losing.
  • Porter Martone (rookie) and Garnet Hathaway (shorthanded) — Philly's goals came from exactly the kind of depth Pittsburgh can't match
  • Dan Vladar's shutout wasn't heroic. It was the logical outcome of a system that gave up almost nothing from the middle.
  • Pittsburgh's third-period push produced shots but no sustained threat. Pressure ≠ danger.
Pittsburgh is playing more. Philadelphia is playing smarter. That's why they're up 2–0.
Pittsburgh's Path
Simplify. Get pucks inside, to the net-front — cross-ice plays against this structure are a trap. Fix the power play entries immediately. Consider line adjustments: Crosby needs help his current linemates can't give.
Philadelphia's Path
Keep doing it. The system is working exactly as designed. No changes required — just patience, discipline, and waiting for the next Pittsburgh mistake.
Series Outlook · Updated
After Game 1 we said "coin flip." After Game 2, it's not. The Flyers are firmly in control. If Pittsburgh can't generate real interior offense, this ends in 5.
Series 01 · East  ·  Confirmed

Ottawa at Carolina

Series Standing
Hurricanes 2 — Senators 0
  • Carolina 3, Ottawa 2 — in double overtime. A trench battle from the opening faceoff, when the captains fought three seconds in.
  • Low-event, high-pressure, low-margin. Few Grade A chances either way. The kind of game where inches decide everything.
  • The turning moment: Ottawa's goal overturned on review — Andersen gloved the puck before it crossed the line. Stays 1–0 Carolina instead of 1–1.
  • Ottawa's late push was real — sustained pressure, PP looks, a 6-on-4 advantage. Andersen didn't let any of it through.
  • Carolina's goals came from exactly where playoff goals come from. Net-front chaos, rebounds, traffic. Stankoven opened it; Hall was in the mess for the insurance.
  • Andersen's 22 saves weren't about volume. They were about timing. The overturned goal. The scramble save on Tkachuk. Elite goaltending is knowing which saves matter.
Ottawa stayed in the fight. Carolina won the moments that decide playoff games.
Ottawa's Path
Generate more interior offense. Net-front traffic earlier, not as a third-period adjustment. Finish the power play chances — they'll get fewer, not more, from here. Watch Zub's status; depth will matter.
Carolina's Path
Don't change anything. The system works. The goalie is locked in. Keep forcing low-quality shots and wait for the mistake — it always comes.
Series Outlook · Updated
We said Carolina in 5 or 6 after Game 1. Nothing has happened to change that. If anything, the double-OT survival win tightens it — Ottawa can hang, they just can't solve Andersen. Carolina in 5.
§ 03

Trends hardening.

One data point is a theory. Two is a pattern. Here's what Game 2 turned into pattern.

TREND / 01

Structure beats possession.

  • Pittsburgh doubled Philly in shot attempts — and lost 3–0
  • Ottawa couldn't generate Grade A chances all night — and lost in 2OT
  • Three games, three times the structured team beat the chasing team. Territorial dominance is a losing metric without finish.
TREND / 02

Goaltending is deciding series.

  • Andersen's overturned-goal save was the difference in a 3–2 double-OT win
  • Vladar shut out Pittsburgh while his team got outshot nearly 2-to-1
  • Not volume saves — timing saves. The goalie who makes one impossible stop at the right moment is worth more than 40 ordinary ones.
TREND / 03

Veteran teams keep showing up late.

  • Carolina survived 2OT without blinking. Vegas closed Utah in the third. Philly closed Pittsburgh by never letting it open.
  • The experienced teams aren't panicking when tied, trailing, or dragged into overtime
  • The younger teams are either surging (Montreal) or collapsing (Utah, Pittsburgh, Ottawa in key moments)

The Take

§ 02

Buffalo–Boston, Game 4.

A 6–1 final that was decided in twenty minutes. Buffalo finally produced a complete game — and Boston's structural problems aren't fixable in 48 hours.

Series 06 · East  ·  Stranglehold

Buffalo at Boston

Series Standing
Sabres 3 — Bruins 1
  • Buffalo 6, Boston 1. The numbers tell the story: 4–0 after one period. 19–5 in shots. Ten Boston giveaways in the first 20 minutes. By the end of one, this game was over.
  • Three of Buffalo's four first-period goals came directly off forced turnovers during Boston breakouts. This isn't variance — it's a structural failure that the Bruins' top defensive pair (McAvoy and Lindholm) couldn't solve.
  • The Sabres' youngest line — Doan (24), Östlund (22), Benson (20) — drove the game from the drop of the puck. They didn't play together in the regular season. None had played a playoff game before this series. They've found chemistry through style, not familiarity.
  • Twelve Sabres recorded a point. Four had multi-point games. That's a depth signal that's hard to fake — the kind of stat that says a team is deeper than its star chart suggests.
  • Bowen Byram is now the Sabres' most valuable player through four games. Three goals in the postseason. On ice for four of six in this game. The kind of defenseman performance that shifts series outcomes.
  • Two more pieces of bad news for Boston: Arvidsson left after just 3:13 of ice time with an upper-body injury (he was their second-best forward in Game 2's lone win), and the Sabres' power play finally scored after 35 dry chances dating back to the regular season.
Buffalo's speed and tenacity on the forecheck have started to wear down a Boston team that struggles breaking the puck out to begin with. — Fluto Shinzawa & Matthew Fairburn, The Athletic
Boston's Path
Not small tweaks — structural adjustments. Shorter breakout routes. More chip-and-retrieve, less stretch passing. McAvoy and Lindholm have to fight the puck less, support each other more. Possible defensive pairing reset. None of this happens cleanly in 48 hours.
Buffalo's Path
Don't get cute. Forecheck. Forecheck. Forecheck. Stay with Lyon in net. Keep rolling four lines. Keep Byram on the ice. The young line is the secret weapon — let them eat the matchups Boston tries to use against the top six.
Series Outlook · Updated
Sabres in 5. The Bruins' problems aren't about effort or coaching — they're about whether their structure can hold up against speed, and through four games, it can't. Boston's top defensive pair needs to solve Buffalo's forecheck inside 48 hours. That's not a realistic ask.
§ 03

The verdicts landing.

Round 1 is starting to deliver final answers. The patterns we identified in Days 1–3 are now hardening into series-deciding truths.

TREND / 01

Speed beats structure when structure cracks.

  • Buffalo: forecheck overwhelmed Boston's breakout. Up 3–1.
  • Utah: speed broke Vegas's structure. Up 2–1.
  • Anaheim: chaos exposed Edmonton's defensive coverage. Up 2–1.
  • The teams playing fast are beating the teams that needed to be perfect. Three favourites trailing because their structure couldn't survive pressure.
TREND / 02

Depth scoring is the contender signal.

  • Buffalo: 12 players with a point in Game 4. Four multi-point.
  • Carolina: Stankoven's third line drove the sweep. Jarvis pointless.
  • Colorado: 3–0 with MacKinnon and Makar combining for 2 points.
  • Philly: fourth line scored two Game 3 goals.
  • Every Round 1 leader is winning with depth. Every Round 1 trailer is losing without it.
TREND / 03

The young cores have arrived.

  • Buffalo's Doan-Östlund-Benson line: youngest on the team, drove Game 4
  • Anaheim's Sennecke (20), Carlsson (21): scored 42 seconds apart in Game 3
  • Utah's Cooley, Guenther: emerging as the series' difference-makers
  • Montreal's Hutson: OT winner in Game 3, 21 years old
  • Round 1's signature isn't superstars — it's twenty-somethings playing fearlessly.

What to watch tonight.

Three live games, three different series shapes. By midnight, two more series could be functionally over.

✓

1–0 in the second on LA's ice. If they hold, that's the second sweep of Round 1 and Round 2 Bound count ticks to 2. If LA somehow steals one, Colorado heads back to Denver with a chance to close at home — but the perfection narrative breaks.

Edmonton at home, 6:30pm, facing a 3–1 hole. The injury question hasn't been answered. If the Oilers lose tonight, they're done — and McDavid finishes the series with a single goal, an assist, and one of the worst playoff stretches of his career.

Lohrei or Hagens back in the lineup? Lindholm and McAvoy stay paired, or split? Sturm has 48 hours to decide whether to make changes that probably won't save the series — or stay the course and face the firing squad on Tuesday.

The Take
Round 1 is delivering verdicts now. Buffalo's speed beats Boston's structure. Carolina's discipline beat Ottawa's effort. Colorado's patience is beating LA's identity. The favourites that survive are the ones whose system held up — and the favourites who don't are watching their identity get torn apart, one breakout at a time.
